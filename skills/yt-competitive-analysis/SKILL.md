# YouTube Competitive Analysis

## Mandatory Content Standards

- Write in active voice.
- Use short sentences and plain language.
- Address the user as "you" and "your."
- Use `.marketing-os/product-context.md` when it exists. If it is blank or missing, recommend running `product-marketing` first.
- Do not invent view counts, retention data, CTR, audience demographics, or channel analytics.
- Separate public observations from hypotheses.
- Use source URLs and dates when available.
- Respect YouTube terms, creator rights, and fair-use limits.
- Do not use em dashes, hashtags, emojis, or filler closings.
- End every full deliverable with one specific next step.

## Objective

Use this skill to understand what works in a YouTube niche, why certain videos outperform channel baselines, and how you can build stronger titles, thumbnails, topics, and content angles.

Next step: choose three to five competitor channels or one target niche.

## Research Inputs

| Input | Why It Matters |
|-------|----------------|
| Channel or niche | Defines comparison set |
| Goal | Growth, launch, packaging, content ideas, authority, or leads |
| Target viewer | Prevents generic topic analysis |
| Competitor list | Focuses research |
| Time window | Controls relevance |
| Your channel baseline | Enables outlier analysis |

Next step: define the baseline before calling a video an outlier.

## Outlier Method

An outlier video performs far above the channel’s normal range. Do not compare a small channel directly to a giant channel. Compare each video to its own channel baseline.

| Video | Channel | Views | Channel Baseline | Outlier Multiple | Age | Topic | Packaging Pattern |
|-------|---------|-------|------------------|------------------|-----|-------|-------------------|

Outlier signals include high views relative to subscriber count, high views relative to recent channel average, fast early view growth, repeated topic patterns, strong comment intent, and clear search or browse appeal.

Next step: group outliers by topic and packaging pattern.

## Packaging Analysis

Analyze title and thumbnail together.

| Element | Questions |
|---------|-----------|
| Title promise | What outcome, curiosity gap, conflict, or transformation appears? |
| Specificity | Does the title name a time, number, audience, tool, mistake, or result? |
| Thumbnail focus | Is there one clear visual idea? |
| Contrast | Does the thumbnail read at mobile size? |
| Viewer tension | What unanswered question makes the viewer click? |
| Trust | Does the packaging feel credible for the niche? |

Next step: write three title and thumbnail hypotheses from the strongest pattern.

## Content Gap Analysis

| Gap Type | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Beginner gap | Competitors assume knowledge your audience lacks |
| Buyer gap | Videos educate but do not help purchase decisions |
| Implementation gap | Videos explain concepts but skip steps |
| Comparison gap | Viewers need tool, product, or method comparisons |
| Freshness gap | Top videos cover outdated advice |
| Persona gap | Content ignores one high-value segment |

Next step: turn each gap into a video promise.
